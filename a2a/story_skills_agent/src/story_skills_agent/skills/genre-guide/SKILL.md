# Genre Guide

When asked to write in a specific genre, apply these conventions:

## Fantasy
- **Tone**: Sense of wonder and discovery
- **Pacing**: Gradual worldbuilding early, escalating action in later chapters
- **Must-haves**: A magic system (even if subtle), a quest or journey, clear stakes
- **Tropes to embrace**: Chosen one, ancient prophecy, mentor figure, magical artifacts
- **Tropes to subvert**: Make the "chosen one" earn their role through failure first

## Science Fiction
- **Tone**: Speculative and thought-provoking
- **Pacing**: Front-load the world's rules, then explore consequences
- **Must-haves**: A "what if" premise grounded in extrapolated science or technology
- **Tropes to embrace**: First contact, AI consciousness, time paradoxes
- **Avoid**: Technobabble without narrative purpose

## Mystery
- **Tone**: Suspenseful with measured revelation
- **Pacing**: Plant clues in chapters 1-2, red herrings in the middle, reveal at the end
- **Must-haves**: A central question, fair clues the reader can follow, a satisfying answer
- **Structure**: Each chapter should eliminate one possibility while opening another

## Thriller
- **Tone**: Urgent and high-stakes
- **Pacing**: Short chapters, cliffhangers, ticking clocks
- **Must-haves**: An antagonist with a credible plan, escalating danger, a protagonist under pressure
- **Technique**: End every chapter mid-action or with a revelation

## Romance
- **Tone**: Emotionally rich, character-driven
- **Pacing**: Slow build of tension, push-pull dynamics, satisfying resolution
- **Must-haves**: Two compelling leads, a believable obstacle, emotional payoff
- **Beats**: Meet-cute, growing connection, conflict/separation, reconciliation
