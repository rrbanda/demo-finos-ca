# Story Structure Frameworks

When planning a story's arc, choose the most appropriate framework:

## Three-Act Structure (Default)

**Act 1 — Setup (25%)**
- Introduce the protagonist in their ordinary world
- Establish the central conflict or inciting incident
- End with a turning point that locks the protagonist into the story

**Act 2 — Confrontation (50%)**
- Rising action: obstacles escalate, stakes increase
- Midpoint reversal: a twist that changes the protagonist's approach
- Low point: the protagonist faces their greatest setback

**Act 3 — Resolution (25%)**
- Climax: the final confrontation
- Falling action: immediate consequences
- Resolution: new equilibrium established

## Hero's Journey (For epic/adventure stories)

1. **Ordinary World** — Establish normalcy
2. **Call to Adventure** — The disruption
3. **Refusal of the Call** — Hesitation or fear
4. **Meeting the Mentor** — Guidance appears
5. **Crossing the Threshold** — Commitment to the journey
6. **Tests, Allies, Enemies** — The bulk of the adventure
7. **The Ordeal** — The central crisis
8. **The Reward** — What was gained
9. **The Return** — Coming back changed

## In Medias Res (For thrillers/mysteries)

- Open in the middle of action or at a dramatic moment
- Use the first chapter to hook, then flash back to explain
- Gradually converge the timeline back to the opening scene
- The reader's curiosity about "how did we get here?" drives the narrative

## Application Rules

1. For short stories (3 chapters), compress the three-act structure: Ch1=Setup, Ch2=Confrontation, Ch3=Resolution
2. Always identify the story's central question and ensure every chapter advances it
3. Each chapter needs its own mini-arc: goal, conflict, outcome
